# Sonye — marketing brief (v1 launch)

Single source of truth for anyone (human or AI) writing marketing for sonye.app.
If copy contradicts this file, the copy is wrong.

## One-liner
Curated pickleball games with reliable groups, at Dallas public courts.

## Elevator (3 sentences)
Sonye posts small pickleball games at Dallas parks — exactly six players, skill-
tagged, free. You pick a game, verify your phone, and show up; the group is locked,
so nobody flakes into a 40-person pile-on and nobody plays alone. No profiles to
build, no fees, no app to download — it's a link.

## The promise (the only promise)
Curated games. Reliable groups. Dallas. That is the entire v1 claim surface —
every ad, post, and page must be satisfiable by a six-person pickleball game at a
Dallas public court this week.

## Audience + insight
Solo players and new-to-Dallas people who already want to play but bounce off the
two existing options: the physical paddle-rack queue at public courts (genuinely
fails solo players — you wait, you get odd-manned, you leave) and flaky group
chats (40 people, 5 show). The insight from field research: the demand exists at
the courts already; what's missing is a reliable *group*, not awareness of the sport.

## Positioning — name the real enemy
The competitor is the paddle rack and the group chat, never other apps. Frame
every message as the fix for "showed up and couldn't get a game" or "planned a
game and half the chat ghosted." Do not mention or compare to other products.

## Voice
Warm, plain, a little understated. Sentence case everywhere. No exclamation
marks, ever. Contractions welcome. Lowercase wordmark: sonye. CTAs are verb-first
and name the outcome ("See this week's games", "Join this game"). Never salesy,
never corporate, never sporty-bro.

## True proof points (the complete list of claims you may make)
- Games are exactly six players and lock when full.
- Every game is skill-tagged: beginner, advanced beginner, intermediate, advanced.
- Free, at Dallas public courts: Cole Park, Churchill Park, Lake Highlands North.
- Real recurring slots: Saturday 10 am at Cole Park; Thursday 7 pm at Churchill Park.
- Join = pick a game + verify your phone. No passwords, no profile to build.
- Each game has a private chat — only the six of you; it closes two hours after.
- Real first names, phone-verified people. No bots, no ghosts on the roster.
- It's a website (sonye.app) — nothing to install; game links open straight to
  the game.

## Hard bans (auto-reject any draft containing these)
- "Casual sports" plural, multi-sport, multi-city, or "coming to your city" —
  v1 is pickleball, Dallas, full stop.
- Invented testimonials, invented stats, or inflated numbers. Only true numbers
  (currently: 12 players this week · 3 parks · 6 per game). Real quotes only,
  with permission.
- Any host attribution: "I curate", "our founder", "your host". Voice is always
  "curated games" — games belong to the group.
- Stock photos or AI images of people. Real photos of real Sonye games only;
  until those exist, no people imagery at all.
- Exclamation marks, "please", "simply/just/easy", "unlock/seamless/leverage".
- Promises of auto-matching, player hosting, leagues, ratings, or paid features.
- Targeting minors. Link sonye.app/privacy and sonye.app/terms where a channel
  expects it.

## Growth motion (how Sonye actually spreads)
Player-as-voucher beats owner-as-promoter. The unit of marketing is a player
texting a game link (sonye.app/slot/…) to a friend — links unfurl with the game
and are viewable logged-out; the phone-verify wall appears only at Join. Marketing's
job is to start that loop (fill the first games), not replace it. The homepage
(sonye.app) is the cold-visitor pitch and already carries the canonical copy.

## Canonical copy strings (riff on these; don't reinvent the voice)
- Manifesto head: "Join a squad, without the group chat."
- Hero pattern: "[day], [time], [park], your squad." (coral on "your squad.")
- Tagline motif: "Find your squad." — the period is coral; the dot is a brand gesture.
- Paddle FAQ: "Bring a paddle if you have one. If you don't, let your squad know."
- Closing: "Your next game is a tap away."

## Brand kit
- Mark: lowercase Baloo 2 extra-bold "s" in ink with a coral period, on a wash
  tile ("the shortest possible wordmark"). Live as favicon/app icon at sonye.app.
- Type: Baloo 2 (display) + Nunito Sans (body).
- Colors: wash #E6F0FF (bg) · ink #14304D · coral #EE5E00 (actions/accents only)
  · steel #5E80A3 (large secondary) · steel-aa #4A6B8C (small secondary text) ·
  cards #FFFFFF with #CFE0F4 borders.
- Sharing sonye.app anywhere auto-unfurls the official OG card (mark + wordmark
  + subline) — never attach a homemade preview image.
