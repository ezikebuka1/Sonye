## Who Sonye Actually Competes With

**Not other apps.** The real competitor is the physical paddle rack — the free, walk-up,
show-up-and-hope system at the public courts. v1 wins by proving "booked group of 6,
three days out" beats "show up and hope."

**The actual app incumbent at Dallas public courts:** TeamReach — group-code-based, passed
around physically at courts. Its weakness: closed silos. A player in the "Cole 8AM" group
is blind to a game forming at Churchill. TeamReach makes you find the group before you can
find the game. Sonye inverts that — the game is the thing; the group forms around it.
That's the core positioning line.

**Other categories, briefly:**

| Category | Example | Weakness |
|----------|---------|----------|
| Paid club software | CourtReserve, Playbypoint | Too heavy for public play |
| Social networks | Reclub | Fragments users into clubs; Identity Tax |
| Frictionless sharing | Partiful, Swish | No discovery — organizer must bring the crowd |
| General events | Meetup, Facebook | Huge reach but no accountability; flake culture |

---

## What This Confirmed

**The one-game-per-day rule (D9) is a competitive wedge.** Meetup and Facebook groups die
from phantom RSVPs — no consequence for no-shows. D9 was designed to protect realness,
but the research identifies it as Sonye's strongest positioning: the only platform where
an RSVP actually means a game happens. Worth using in marketing copy.

**SMS-first / web-first onboarding is right.** Partiful and Swish validate that
no-app-download, phone-based entry is the winning low-friction path. Confirms the
Partiful Model already in the brief.

**Don't build social profiles or discovery features.** Reclub's "Identity Tax" (heavy
profiles, mandatory photos) is exactly what makes casual players bounce. Confirms the
brief's no-profile-pictures / minimal-onboarding stance.

**Density over segmentation.** The research independently reaches the same conclusion as
"Density Over Everything." External corroboration of the core v1 principle.
