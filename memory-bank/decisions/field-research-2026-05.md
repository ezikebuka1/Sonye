# Field Research Integration — May 2026

> Source: ethnographic observation at Dallas pickleball courts (Cole, Churchill, Fretz, Friendship, Lake Highlands North, plus Sienna/Houston), May 1–16, 2026. Field research is a decision-input, not a decision — each finding was tested against the V1 Decision Triangle, then accepted, rejected, or deferred.

## Findings that confirmed existing strategy (no change)

**Group size 6 is right.** Observed: 5-player groups isolate one person socially ("Odd Pod"); 6 means the two sitting out form a pair. Confirms D7's existing capacity-6 call, adds a social-geometry reason on top of the original flake-absorption reason.

**Loose skill tiers are right.** Pickleball self-regulates skill — tired strong players play down, advanced players sandbag to practice. Confirms not building a strict rating system. Keep the broad 4-tier picker.

**Liquidity beats preferences.** Players abandon "I want competitive" the moment a casual game offers immediate court time. Strong real-world support for "Density Over Everything" and for keeping segmentation filters out of v1.

**The core problem is real.** The physical paddle-rack queue genuinely fails solo players (luck-based, awkward, traps them in bad games). Confirms why the app exists.

## Findings we rejected for v1 (deferred to v2)

**Hyper-Spontaneous "Games Today" home screen.** Field showed players act on impulse, day-of. Rejected for v1 because impulse *at the park* isn't proof people would trust an app at 2pm to produce a game by 4pm — and v1 lacks the density for it. Parked as the strongest signal for the eventual "Play Now" mechanic.

**Duo Pairing, Vetted Network, Sub-In.** Each is a real observed behavior (solos pairing up, players exchanging numbers after good games, bailing for real-life friends). All rejected for v1 as feature-heavy and density-neutral or social-graph-shaped. All parked in v2-signals.md with revisit triggers.

## The finding that changed v1 structurally

**Fretz Park is not an open-play court.** Observed May 13: paid ($5/person), reservation-gated (third-party app, 24h advance), physical gatekeeper, pre-formed groups only, zero solo players, zero cross-court mixing ("Reserved Bubble"). This broke a foundational brief assumption (three interchangeable public courts).

**Resolution:** Fretz removed from v1. May 16 follow-up scouted replacements and verified **Lake Highlands North Park** as a true open-play court (free, high visibility, clustered courts, single universal queue). v1 launches Cole / Churchill / Lake Highlands North. Full reasoning in projectbrief amendment projectbrief-A1.

**Lasting output: the Public Commons Test.** Any venue must pass three checks before inclusion — zero financial barrier, low psychological friction (visible, not "owned-feeling"), single egalitarian queue. Fretz failed #1; Friendship Park (free but hidden in a subdivision, felt like trespassing) failed #2. This is now a reusable venue filter, not a one-time call.

**Case Study #1:** The project owner personally walked up to Fretz with exact target-user intent (solo, spontaneous) and bounced at the desk/payment friction. Strongest possible data point — if the founder won't navigate it, users won't.

## Findings that shaped the UX revisions

The onboarding-form bifurcation (D7.2) was triggered by the realization — surfaced through this research lens — that a user who taps a link to join a specific game has already revealed their availability and venue preference by the act of joining. Asking it again in a form violates the frictionless model. Detail in D7.2.
